The iPhone 6 was first introduced to the public September 14, 2014, featuring a larger display, faster processor, upgraded cameras, and NFC support - in essence, the iPhone 6 introduced a newer, higher performing and more functional smartphone from the world's leading technology company. The launch of the iPhone 6 (and its sibling, the iPhone 6 Plus) signaled the biggest technological lead forward in the iPhone model since the release of the original in 2007.

The expansion of the iPhone 6's new screen was introduced as a response to the market, as new competitors were succeeding by offering new, larger displays than previous iPhone models. While the iPhone 5 featured a taller screen, it remained the same width as previous models. The iPhone 6, on the other hand, was a nod to the changes in the market, as it featured a significantly larger 4.7-inch Retina HD Display.

In addition to the larger screen size, the iPhone 6 also introduced NFC support through a mobile payments system called Apple Pay, upgraded LTE and Wi-Fi connectivity, the ability to shoot video in 30, 60, 120, and 240 frames per second, and a repositioned power button at the top of the phone.

Despite all the technological advancements, the iPhone 6 is far from an indestructible device. It's as easy to break or damage as any other mobile device on the market.
